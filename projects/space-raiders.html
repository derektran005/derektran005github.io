Projects | Space Raiders

(Python/PixelPad)

Project Overview

Space Raiders is a top-down 2D roguelike shooter emphasizing inertia-based movement, modular weapon systems, and state-driven enemy behavior in an infinite, mapless environment.

Project Goals

The goal of this project was to explore non-traditional movement and combat mechanics by simulating space-like inertia, weapon recoil, and system-driven player decision-making.

Engineering Challenges

Balancing inertia-based movement with player control required careful tuning of acceleration, rotational velocity, damping, and weapon recoil to avoid disorientation while preserving the intended “slippery” feel.

Designing enemy behavior that reacts to the player’s aim during reload states required decoupling movement logic from attack logic and introducing predictive avoidance behavior.

Core Systems

Movement & Physics

  • Velocity-based movement with inertia
  • Gradual ship rotation toward cursor direction
  • Knockback forces applied by enemies and weapon recoil

Camera System

  • Multiple weapon archetypes (autocannon, minigun, shotgun)
  • Recoil and rotational velocity applied per shot
  • Shotgun: Charged attacks affecting weapon spread
  • Explosion-based secondary damage effects

Damage System

  • Armor-based damage mitigation
  • Multiple damage types interacting with enemy defenses
  • Ammo and weapon constraints encouraging situational swapping

Enemy AI

  • Advanced enemy moveemnt using positional and rotational velocities
  • Ranged enemies with finite-state machines:
  • Attack state

    • Pursues player and fires ranged projectiles when in range

  • Reload state

    • Evasive behavior, avoiding player's aim

This project reflects a long-term iterative development process, where new mechanics were layered onto existing systems while preserving stability and player control.
